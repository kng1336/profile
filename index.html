Dear Vietnam School of Science Officials,
My interest in molding things into being started with clay and toothpicks. It soon matured into a fascination with artificial intelligence and the means became programming. By 15, I had completed half of the University of Helsinki’s massive open online course (MOOC) on the programming language of Java. I looked to HTML and CSS’ shaping ability as a necessary recourse and completed freeCodeCamp’s Responsive Web Development (RWD) during the preparation for the VSSS. Now with the same passion burning in me, I write to apply for the Vietnam Summer School of Science.

I have earned prizes in test-based as well as rhetorical competitions at the city and provincial level to show for my proficiency in English. I have surrounded myself with peers with the same passion for the language for one year at Quoc Hoc high school for the gifted. I am proud of my academic achievements all round with English at the center. At heart, however, I see the subject purely as a tool. And a tool it is. My aptitude for understanding and communicating in English will come in handy when participating in this year’s VSSS.

During my two-month-long Covid break, I began learning Java programming with a MOOC organized by the University of Helsinki. It took me one year of practice to complete the first part of the program. I can now code a timer, a note keeper, an open dictionary, and a christmas tree generator - all object-oriented. Beyond that, I gained basic understanding of Java, and am now able to read and integrate code from StackOverflow and such. Java is the language of my choosing to mold my intelligence from the ground up. And VSSS is my choice to learn about the nature of AI and the methodology of how I should go about learning it.

Looking at web development’s HTML and CSS’ straightforward working mechanism, I self-studied marking down and up styling websites. In completing freeCodeCamp’s web development course, I ensured a proof for my programming ability to compute and make a website. I believe this effort will go towards shaping the exoskeleton of the AI I desire to create.

I have participated in many extracurricular activities, and am confident in my ability to study and have a good time. I would love to be considered for this year’s VSSS.

Sincerely,
Nguyen Quang Khiem
